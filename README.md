# 📚 Goodreads — Market Analysis Dashboard

> A market intelligence report analysing Goodreads' book catalogue across author performance, genre benchmarking, and content gap identification — structured as a publisher's strategic brief.

---

## Project Summary

This project treats the Goodreads catalogue as a real publisher engagement — the kind a marketing or business analyst at Book Company  would run to guide acquisition, promotion, and investment decisions. Every visualisation answers a specific business or marketing question, and every question leads to a decision recommendation.

**Dataset:** 13,507 books · 5,543 authors · 490M ratings · Genres, page counts, publication years, avg ratings, ratings count · Goodreads Works

---

## Analytical Framework

All questions are structured across four tiers used in professional business analysis:

| Tier | What it answers | Focus |
|------|----------------|-------|
| **Descriptive** | What the market looks like | Volume, reach, genre distribution |
| **Diagnostic** | Why certain patterns exist | Quality vs reach divergence, author concentration |
| **Predictive** | What signals to act on | Engagement rate as leading indicator |
| **Prescriptive** | What publishers should do | Investment prioritisation, gap strategy |

## Questions Answered

### Business Analyst — Portfolio & Market Structure

- **D-01** Which genres command the highest total ratings, and what does genre reach concentration reveal about where reader demand is actually pooled?
- **D-02** How is the author base distributed across total ratings — and what does the Pareto curve reveal about market concentration risk for publishers?
- **D-03** How does average book rating vary across page-count ranges — and what does this reveal about reader satisfaction by content depth?
- **D-04** What is the publication year trend in average ratings — and does recency correlate with quality or with rating inflation?
- **DG-01** Why do Fiction and Young-Adult dominate reach while Comics and Graphic novels lead quality — what is the volume-quality divergence mechanism?
- **DG-02** Why does J.K. Rowling's total ratings (144M) outnumber the next author (Dan Brown, 14M) by 10× — franchise depth or audience loyalty?
- **P-01** Based on engagement rate and avg rating signals, which authors represent the strongest forward investment candidates for publishers?
- **PR-01** Which genre segments show high quality but low reach — and where should publishers place acquisition bets to capture underserved audiences?
- **PR-02** What author prioritisation framework should guide promotional spend decisions — and what minimum thresholds qualify an author for investment?

---

### Marketing Analyst — Audience, Engagement & Positioning

- **D-05** Which genres carry the highest average reader rating — and how does this separate niche-loyalty segments from mass-market segments?
- **D-06** Who are the top authors by average rating vs total ratings — and what does the divergence between these two lists reveal about brand vs volume?
- **DG-03** What is the mechanism behind high engagement rate authors like Jojo Moyes (9.5) and Rainbow Rowell (9.0) — volume of reads or depth of reader recurrence?
- **DG-04** Why do books over 700 pages achieve the highest average ratings (4.1) — is this a quality signal, a selection effect, or a committed-reader bias?
- **P-02** Can author engagement rate — combining ratings volume and recurrence signals — serve as a leading indicator for publisher investment decisions ahead of lagging revenue metrics?
- **PR-03** How should a publisher structure genre-author combination campaigns to maximise ratings reach — and which pairings show the strongest combined signal?
- **PR-04** What content gap strategy should guide new title acquisition — identifying high-quality, low-volume segments where new entries face less competition but capture loyal audiences?

---

## Key Findings

- **Fiction leads reach at 0.49bn ratings** — but Comics and Graphic Novels lead average quality at ~4.0+. Volume and quality do not live in the same segment. Publishers optimising for reach and those optimising for loyalty need different genre strategies.
- **Pareto concentration confirmed:** ~20% of authors account for ~80% of cumulative ratings. The top author alone (J.K. Rowling, 144M) generates more ratings than the next five authors combined — signalling extreme dependency on franchise-driven talent.
- **J.K. Rowling's avg rating (4.4) is the highest among top-volume authors** — she is the only author who simultaneously leads on reach AND quality, making her an outlier case study in franchise-brand compounding.
- **Books over 700 pages average 4.1 ratings** vs 3.9 for books under 100 pages — longer content consistently scores higher, supporting investment in series and long-form formats over standalone short reads.
- **Custom Engagement Rate metric designed** — combining ratings volume and recurrence signals to surface authors like Jojo Moyes (9.5) and Rainbow Rowell (9.0) who outperform on reader loyalty even without J.K. Rowling-level raw numbers.
- **Content gap identified** in the high-avg-rating / low-ratings-count quadrant — genres with strong quality scores but low market penetration represent whitespace for new publisher acquisitions with lower competitive noise.
